Close Reading: Theories of Representation by J. Kidd

A close reading on a chapter titled Theories of Representation, written by J. Kidd in which they discuss the theory around meaning making, signs and symbols and the weight and influence that they have in terms of structuring our realities and enforcing the way that peopl engage with and experience their relaties. Drawing on theorists like Ferdidnand de Saussure, Roland Barthes and other such theorists.

Data Visualisation: A History

A history of data visualisation as told by Michael Friendly, detailing the earliest known practices of data visualisation, like those done in Egypt as far back as 200 BC. Friendly documents data visualisation through the centuries, marking the milestones, successes and pitfalls encountered all throughout history to the present day and the innovations that have made data visualisation into what we know it as today.

Janssen Essay – Sabine Roeser: How Art Can Contribute to Ethical Reflection on Risky Technologies

a close reading of Sabine Roeser's essay on 'techno-art' and the influence and impact that it has on the discourse around risky technologies. Roeser explores the ways in which art as a medium offers new perspectives and makes real the situations that contexts around which these technologies are used as well as thinking and utilising these technologies in ways that are not considered the norm.

Fernanda B. Viégas and Martin Wattenberg: Artistic Data Visualization: Beyond Visual Analytics

A close reading on Viégas and Wattenberg's article on the concept of artistic visualisation in terms of it's origins and looking at examples of these artworks and how and why they are different from traditional visualisations.
